## Option A: Deep Dive

### Product: Audit Prep + DSR + Evidence

**Core Features (MVP):**
1. **Compliance Checklist** - GDPR/CCPA/HIPAA templates, 3 minutes to import
2. **Evidence Dashboard** - Link Google Drive, Slack, Notion docs; auto-tag to checklist items
3. **DSR Workflow** - Intake form ‚Üí route to teams ‚Üí deadline tracking ‚Üí proof of delivery
4. **Audit Report** - One-click PDF/Word export, audit-ready format
5. **Team Roles** - Privacy Officer assigns tasks to Legal, IT, Department heads
6. **Slack Integration** - Notify on DSR arrivals, task updates, audit reminders
7. **Mobile Logger** - Quick-capture for incidents (on-the-go)

**Build Time:** 3 months (solo founder with 1 engineer hired at Month 2)

**MVP Launch:** Week 12 with 3-5 design partners on-boarded

### Market Fit

**ICP Profile:**
- **Title:** Privacy Officer / Chief Privacy Officer / Compliance Manager
- **Company:** 50-300 employees, $5M-$100M ARR
- **Industry:** Tech, Healthcare, SaaS, Finance (anywhere GDPR/CCPA is relevant)
- **Pain:** 20+ hours/week on manual audit prep, DSR tracking, evidence collection
- **Annual budget:** $10K-$20K compliance tech (separate from legal tech, security tools)

**Buying Committee:**
- **Champion:** Privacy Officer (day-to-day pain)
- **Economic Buyer:** General Counsel or VP Legal (budget approval)
- **IT Veto:** CISO (security/data handling concerns)
- **End Users:** Compliance team, Legal, IT

**Trigger Events (when they buy):**
1. Audit notice arrives (30-60 day SLA to prepare)
2. New Privacy Officer hired (mandate to modernize)
3. DSR volume spikes (existing manual process breaks)
4. Compliance incident / near-miss (urgency to tighten controls)
5. Funding round or acquisition (auditors scrutinize)

## Why Not Option B (DSR-Only)?

**Pros:**
- Faster build (6-8 weeks vs 3 months)
- Clearer customer pain (DSR compliance is legal mandate)
- Could integrate with specialized platforms (e.g., Didomi for consent management)

**Cons:**
- Narrower market (only companies handling 50+ DSRs/year)
- Product gets commoditized (many DSR tools exist: DPR, Osano)
- Lower founder leverage (requires healthcare/fintech specialization)
- ACV is lower ($12-15K) due to smaller TAM
- Churn risk (once DSR process is set up, customers have low touch)

**Verdict:** Good 2nd product (Year 2), but too risky as MVP. Audit prep is bigger pain.

---

## Why Not Option C (Premium Audit Readiness)?

**Pros:**
- Higher ACV ($15-20K) and upmarket potential
- Could position as "enterprise-grade compliance"
- General Counsel buying power is strong

**Cons:**
- Longer build (4-5 months, UX-heavy)
- Longer sales cycle (90-120 days to GC approval)
- Founder doesn't have GC/enterprise sales background
- Higher CAC ($2K-3K to reach GC vs $530 for Privacy Officer)
- Unit economics suffer (slower close, higher acquisition cost)
- Timing risk: MVP takes too long, competitors ship first

**Verdict:** Good upmarket strategy for Year 2-3, but too risky for MVP.
