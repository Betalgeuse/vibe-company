## üéì KEY LEARNINGS

### 1. Buyer Type Determines GTM Model
- **Tech-savvy buyers (developers)** ‚Üí PLG/Self-Serve works
- **Non-tech buyers (compliance officers, CFOs)** ‚Üí Founder-Led sales required
- **Custody buyers** ‚Üí High-trust = needs expert, long sales cycle

### 2. Capital Requirement Varies Wildly
- **Testing:** $20-30K (data/ops cost almost nothing)
- **Compliance:** $10-20K (minimal infrastructure)
- **Analytics:** $50-100K (blockchain data subscriptions expensive)
- **Infrastructure:** $100K+ (node operations at scale)
- **Custody:** $200K+ (audit + insurance + ops team)

### 3. Regulatory Headwinds Are Double-Edged
- **Positive:** MiCA enforcement = compliance tool demand ‚úÖ
- **Positive:** AML/KYC regulations = compliance urgency ‚úÖ
- **Negative:** MSB licensing = custody barriers too high ‚ùå
- **Negative:** New rules monthly = unpredictable TAM shifts ‚ö†Ô∏è

### 4. Freemium Model Varies by Category
- **Works well:** Developer tools (testing, analytics, infrastructure)
- **Works okay:** SMB vertical analytics (niche communities)
- **Fails:** Compliance tools (liability concerns), Custody (security trust)

### 5. Founder Bottleneck Hits Different Opportunities at Different Points
- **Testing:** Hits at 30-50 customers (then need sales hire)
- **Analytics:** Hits at 500+ customers (longer rope due to freemium)
- **Compliance:** Hits at 25-30 customers (longer sales cycle = fewer customers)
- **Infrastructure:** Hits at 20-25 customers (ops burden escalates)
- **Custody:** Hits at 10-15 customers (complex deal structure)
