## üî¥ MODEL 1: PRODUCT-LED SALES (PLG + Light Sales)
**Score: 5.7/10** | **Status: NOT RECOMMENDED AS PRIMARY**

### Detailed Scoring

#### Product Fit (Can onboarding be self-serve?) - 5/10
**Positive Factors** ‚úÖ:
- Visual dashboard + checklist = immediately understandable value
- Free trial demonstrable (can show with dummy data)
- Low barrier to adoption (staff can figure out basics)

**Negative Factors** ‚ùå:
- IT provisioning integrations NOT self-serve (requires guide)
- Buying committee friction (VP People + CFO can't both trial alone)
- Value requires live hire event (timing dependency; can't trial on-demand)
- Trial-to-paid conversion low for HR SaaS (5-10% benchmark)
- Self-serve users less engaged (churn 40-50% ARR vs 15-20% for high-touch)

**Verdict**: Onboarding software CAN have trials, but has fundamental self-serve friction.

---

#### Sales Cycle Alignment (45-90 days target) - 5/10
**Model Cycle**: 60-120 days (longer than target)

**Why Extends Cycle**:
1. Trial inertia: SMB users start trial but delay decision (low urgency in free trial)
2. No top-of-funnel salesperson: PLG relies on self-initiated demand (unpredictable timing)
3. Buying committee not engaged until decision: VP People trials, but CFO only engages at closing (adds 2-3 weeks)
4. Integration anxiety: Trial users abandon when they hit integration wall (creates extended "consideration" phase)

**Benchmark Comparison**:
- BambooHR (self-serve dominant): 30-45 days
- Rippling (high-touch sales): 90-180 days
- PLG + light sales model: 60-120 days (hybrid, tends toward longer)

---

#### CAC Feasibility (Target <$3K; Realistic $1-1.5K) - 5/10
**Theoretical CAC**: $1-2K  
**Actual CAC**: $2-3K+ (due to low conversion)

**Calculation**:
```
Scenario: PLG-dominant approach
‚îú‚îÄ Product Hunt launch: 100 trials ‚Üí 1% conversion = 1 customer
‚îú‚îÄ Organic search: 0 trials (first month); ramps Month 6
‚îú‚îÄ Paid ads: $500/month ‚Üí 10-15 trials ‚Üí 0.5-1 customer
‚îú‚îÄ Community/referral: 0 (pre-scale)
‚îî‚îÄ Total Month 1: 2-3 customers from ~120 trials
   CAC = (PH effort $0 + ads $500 + founder support) / 2-3 customers
   CAC = $1.5K-2K per customer (assumes founder does light support on 30-50 trials)
```

**Why CAC Creeps**:
1. Trial volume high, but conversion low (1-2%) = need massive volume for revenue
2. Founder support required to convert trials (can't be hands-off) = CAC hidden in founder time
3. Light sales = still requires engagement (SDR follow-up, demo support) = CAC rises
4. Volume-heavy = support burden (community/forum responses, email support)

**Blended CAC at Scale**: $2-3K (not <$1K achievable)

---

#### Founder Effort: Sustainability - 7/10
**Weekly Effort**: 10-15 hours (relatively low)

**Breakdown**:
- Trial follow-up emails: 5 hrs/week (automated templates + manual followup)
- Demo/support calls: 5-10 hrs/week (light touch; only for high-intent trials)
- Operations: 5 hrs/week (customer onboarding, troubleshooting)

**Sustainability**: ‚úÖ YES, sustainable for 12+ months (not exhausting)

**BUT** ‚ùå **Conversion dependency**: If trial-to-paid drops below 5%, founder effort increases (more follow-up needed) = becomes unsustainable.

---

#### Customer Stickiness (Switching costs, churn, expansion) - 6/10
**Switching Cost**: MEDIUM (low investment in trial ‚Üí higher churn risk)

**Churn Rate**: 15-25% ARR (higher than founder-led due to weaker onboarding)

**Expansion Potential**: MEDIUM (trial users less likely to buy add-ons vs high-touch customers)

**Why Stickiness is Mediocre**:
1. Free trial attracts price-sensitive buyers (more likely to churn)
2. Less founder engagement = weaker customer success (defaults to self-serve support)
3. No relationship-based loyalty (trial-to-paid user has no personal connection)

---

#### Time to Scale - 6/10
**Scaling Mechanics**:
- Paid ads (Google, LinkedIn): Can scale 2x/month (budget increase)
- Organic (referral, community): Slow ramp (requires 6+ months reputation building)
- Product Hunt: One-time event (limited repeatable volume)

**Bottleneck**: Paid acquisition (Google Ads) is only scalable channel, but:
- CPCs high for HR keywords ($50-100+)
- Conversion rates declining (declining ROAS after initial winners)
- Not sustainable at scale (GMV efficiency poor for $8K ACV)

**Realistic Scale**:
- Year 1: 20-30 customers (limited by channel efficiency)
- Year 2: 50-70 customers (if paid ads scale, heavy CAC spending)

---

### Critical Risks

1. **Trial-to-paid bottleneck**: If conversion stays at 5%, need 10,000+ trials/month to hit 50 customers ‚Üí unsustainable volume
2. **Free tier cannibalization**: SMBs stick on free tier indefinitely; churn on paid tier (poor LTV)
3. **Support burden scales with trials**: 1,000 trials/month = support burden (forum, email, chat) even if only 10 convert
4. **No buying committee alignment**: VP People trials independently; CFO never sees product ‚Üí surprise objections at close

---

### Verdict
**Recommended**: NO (as primary)  
**Alternative**: Use as secondary channel AFTER founder-led sales establishes PMF (Month 6+)

**Reasoning**: PLG-only will result in low conversion (5-10%), high CAC ($2-3K), and extended sales cycles (90-120 days). Better to combine with founder sales to accelerate conversion.

## üü¢ MODEL 2: FOUNDER-LED SALES
**Score: 7.2/10** | **Status: üèÜ RECOMMENDED PRIMARY**

### Detailed Scoring

#### Product Fit (High-touch required?) - 8/10
**Why Strong Fit**:
1. ‚úÖ IT provisioning complexity = founder credibility required (not self-explanatory)
2. ‚úÖ Buying committee alignment = founder can address both VP People + CFO concerns
3. ‚úÖ Customization expectations = founder can discuss trade-offs (features, timeline)
4. ‚úÖ Integration concerns = founder can provide technical assurance
5. ‚úÖ Trust at stake = "will this break our IT?" = founder relationship key

**Evidence**:
- Rippling's success = founder-led sales for complex product (founder James Ryebak sells strategically)
- BambooHR's sales = "light sales" model (founders involved in enterprise deals, not self-serve only)
- Gusto's success = founder-led for payroll (trust critical)

---

#### Sales Cycle Alignment (45-90 days target) - 8/10
**Model Cycle**: 60-80 days (within target, shorter than baseline)

**Why Founder Accelerates**:
1. Personal relationship = faster trust building (Day 1 vs Week 2)
2. Discovery depth = fewer surprises later (reduces extended evaluation)
3. CFO conversation = founder can do ROI math in real-time (speeds CFO approval)
4. Customization clarity = founder can scope integration work (removes blocker)

**Breakdown**:
```
Week 1-2:  Outreach + initial conversation
Week 3-4:  Demo + trial setup
Week 5-6:  Evaluation + integration testing (founder assist)
Week 7-8:  CFO review + approval (founder presents ROI)
Week 9:    Contract + onboarding
Total:     60-65 days (faster than self-serve 90-120 days)
```

---

#### CAC Feasibility (Target <$3K; Realistic $1-1.5K) - 8/10
**Realistic CAC**: $1K-1.5K ‚úÖ ACHIEVABLE

**Calculation**:
```
Week 1: Founder reaches out to 50 targets (LinkedIn + email)
‚îú‚îÄ LinkedIn (warm intros + cold 1st degree): 30 ‚Üí 5-10 replies (15-30%)
‚îú‚îÄ Cold email: 20 ‚Üí 0-1 replies (0-5%)
‚îú‚îÄ Total replies: 5-11 qualified conversations
‚îî‚îÄ Demo conversion: 50% = 2.5-5.5 demos booked

Week 2-4: Run demos (2-3 per week)
‚îú‚îÄ Demo-to-trial: 75-100% (high-quality leads only)
‚îú‚îÄ Trial-to-paid: 30-50% (founder follow-up assist + product quality)
‚îú‚îÄ Customer conversions: 0.5-1 customer per week = 2-4/month

Month cost:
‚îú‚îÄ LinkedIn Navigator: $500
‚îú‚îÄ Apollo/email tool: $500
‚îú‚îÄ Founder time: 20 hours @ $75/hr opportunity cost = $1,500
‚îú‚îÄ Total cost: $2,500 for 2-4 customers
‚îî‚îÄ CAC = $2,500 √∑ 3 customers = ~$833/customer (scales to $1K-1.5K)
```

**CAC Progression** (as process improves):
- Month 1-2: $1.5K-2K (discovery phase, high effort per deal)
- Month 3-4: $1.2K-1.5K (process tightens, messaging refines)
- Month 5-6: $1K-1.2K (inbound + referral leverage improves)

**Why This Works**: Founder time is high but per-customer conversion is high (30-50% trial-to-paid vs 5-10% PLG-only).

---

#### Founder Effort: Sustainability - 6/10
**Weekly Effort**: 25-30 hours (moderate-to-high)

**Breakdown**:
- Outreach (research, personalization): 5-7 hrs/week
- Demos + calls: 8-10 hrs/week (5 demos √ó 1.5-2 hrs each + prep)
- Trial support + follow-up: 5-8 hrs/week
- Operations: 5-7 hrs/week (onboarding, product work on hold)

**Sustainability Concern**: ‚ö†Ô∏è Unsustainable past Month 6

**Why Burnout Risk**:
1. 25-30 hrs/week sales + product development = no time for strategic work
2. Repetitive demos = mental fatigue (same message 5x/week)
3. Rejection (70-75% no-closes) = emotional toll
4. Pipeline management = requires discipline to avoid feast/famine cycles

**Mitigation**:
- ‚úÖ Hire SDR by Month 7 (non-negotiable)
- ‚úÖ Hire VA Month 3 for research/list (frees 5-7 hrs/week)
- ‚úÖ Time-box at 20 hrs/week (rest on product/ops)

---

#### Customer Stickiness (Switching costs, churn, expansion) - 8/10
**Switching Cost**: MEDIUM-HIGH (founder relationship + data + staff training)

**Churn Rate**: 10-15% ARR (lower than PLG due to high-touch onboarding + relationship)

**Expansion Potential**: HIGH (founder knows customer deeply, can sell add-ons)

**Why Stickiness is Strong**:
1. Personal relationship = harder to churn (founder is relationship anchor)
2. Integration investment = high switching cost (data migration, retraining)
3. Success orientation = founder helps customer succeed (better NPS/retention)
4. Upsell opportunities = founder understands customer needs (knows when to expand)

---

#### Time to Scale - 5/10
**Scaling Mechanics**:
- Founder capacity bottleneck: 5 demos/week = 20-25 demos/month = 7-12 conversions/month (assuming 30-40% close rate)
- Monthly capacity: ~10 new customers/month (founder ceiling)
- Year 1 capacity: 60-80 customers (founder only)

**Reality**: Founder does 5-10 demos/week = 20-40 demos/month = 6-15 conversions = actual 10-20 customers/month realistically

**Bottleneck**: Founder is bottleneck; hit ceiling Month 6 without SDR/BDR hire

**Scale Path**:
1. Month 1-6: Founder does all sales (10-15 customers/month possible)
2. Month 7: SDR hire (scales to 3x capacity)
3. Month 8+: SDR + founder (40-50 customers/month possible)

---

### Critical Risks

1. **Founder burnout (Month 4-6)**: Unsustainable 25-30 hrs/week beyond 6 months
2. **Product dependency**: Weak product = founder can't sell it (no amount of charisma fixes bad UX)
3. **Limited by founder bandwidth**: Can't exceed 10-15 customers/month without SDR
4. **Founder transition risk**: If founder steps back, company loses credibility anchor

---

### Verdict
**Recommended**: YES (as primary, Month 1-6)

**Conditions**:
- ‚úÖ Founder willing to do high-touch sales (not just product work)
- ‚úÖ Product delivers on demo promises
- ‚úÖ SDR hire non-negotiable Month 7
- ‚úÖ Realistic Year 1 target: 25-35 customers (not 100+)

**Reasoning**: Best fit for complex product + high-touch buyer. Founder credibility accelerates cycle. Sustainable only 6 months; plan SDR hire non-negotiable.

## üî¥ MODEL 3: SELF-SERVE PLUS (Freemium + Optional Support)
**Score: 4.5/10** | **Status: NOT RECOMMENDED**

### Detailed Scoring

#### Product Fit (Self-serve friendly?) - 3/10
**Why Poor Fit**:
1. ‚ùå Core differentiator (IT provisioning) locked behind paid tier
2. ‚ùå Free tier becomes "task list app" (not differentiated from basic tools)
3. ‚ùå Value not apparent in free tier (can't show AI assistant without live hire)
4. ‚ùå Compliance requirements (I-9/eVerify) require paid tier = no meaningful free experience
5. ‚ùå Free tier low stickiness (users don't invest time if limited features)

**Free Tier Value Prop Problem**:
- Free users can: Create onboarding checklists, basic workflows, document upload
- Free users can't: Provision IT, use AI assistant, verify I-9, integrate with HRIS
- Result: Free tier looks basic (no differentiation vs Excel/Asana)

---

#### Sales Cycle Alignment (45-90 days target) - 3/10
**Model Cycle**: 120-180 days (very long)

**Why Extended**:
1. Free tier adoption slow (low urgency, voluntary adoption)
2. Value only apparent after 4-8 weeks of active use
3. Upgrade decision late in cycle (only after hitting free tier limits)
4. No relationship anchor (no salesperson driving timeline)

**Benchmark**: Freemium models typical 120-180 day cycle (much longer than target)

---

#### CAC Feasibility (Target <$3K) - 4/10
**Realistic CAC**: $2K-4K+ (POOR)

**Why High**:
```
Scenario: Freemium approach
‚îú‚îÄ Free signups needed: 500-1000/month (to yield 5-10 upgrades)
‚îú‚îÄ Paid conversions: 1-2% of free users = 5-10 upgrades/month
‚îú‚îÄ CAC = marketing cost to drive 500-1000 signups / 5-10 conversions
‚îú‚îÄ CAC = $1,000 (marketing) √∑ 7.5 conversions = $133 each
‚îî‚îÄ BUT: Add support burden (email, chat, community) = hidden CAC cost
   Total implied CAC: $2-4K (support cost amortized)
```

**Why Freemium CAC is Bad**:
1. High volume, low conversion = lots of support for free tier (unmonetized)
2. Churn of free users high (60-80%) = marketing waste
3. Only valuable if growth viral (unlikely for B2B HR tool)

---

#### Founder Effort: Sustainability - 8/10
**Weekly Effort**: 10-12 hours (low founder sales effort)

**Why Low**:
1. Minimal sales team needed (mostly community/support)
2. Growth via product + marketing (not founder demos)
3. Scalable without founder (unlike founder-led sales)

**BUT** ‚ùå **Support burden**: Freemium models require 24/7 support (forum, chat, community) = unsustainable long-term without CS team hire

---

#### Customer Stickiness (Switching costs, churn, expansion) - 5/10
**Switching Cost**: LOW (free tier users minimal investment)

**Churn Rate**: 40-50% ARR (very high; free tier churn alone is 60-80%, paid tier 10-15%)

**Expansion Potential**: MEDIUM (some free users upgrade, most stay free indefinitely)

**Why Stickiness Poor**:
1. Free tier users have low data investment (easy to abandon)
2. Freemium competition (many free onboarding tools exist)
3. Perceived risk: "If free tier exists, why pay?" (downward pressure on ARPU)

---

#### Time to Scale - 4/10
**Scaling Mechanics**:
- Volume-dependent (need 10,000+ free signups to generate 100-200 paid customers)
- Marketing-driven (need $10-20K/month marketing budget to sustain growth)
- Network effects weak (low viral coefficient for B2B HR tool)

**Reality**: Freemium scales only if:
1. Viral growth (not likely for onboarding tool)
2. Massive marketing budget (unsustainable for bootstrap startup)
3. High paid conversion (if 3-5%, not 1-2%)

---

### Critical Risks

1. **Freemium trap**: 95% users on free tier; only 5% convert (poor unit economics)
2. **Support burden scales with free users**: Free signups = support requests (no revenue)
3. **Cannibalization**: Free tier competes with own paid offering (downward pressure on pricing)
4. **Cash flow problem**: CAC > LTV in early phase (only sustainable at $10M+ ARR scale with brand)

---

### Verdict
**Recommended**: NO

**Alternative**: Only viable if combined with founder-led sales to convert free users (then becomes Model 2 with added friction).

**Reasoning**: Freemium alone creates poor unit economics (1-2% conversion, high support burden). Self-serve alone insufficient for $8K ACV.
