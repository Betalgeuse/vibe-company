## 1. GTM MODELS EVALUATION

### A) Product-Led Sales (PLG + Light Sales Assist)

#### Score: 3.5/10 ‚ùå **NOT RECOMMENDED**

**Rationale:**
- PSA tools historically **fail at PLG** due to complexity and integration requirements
- High switching costs and implementation effort create friction for self-serve trial
- Target ICP (VP Operations, Project Managers) expects consultative selling for $12-25K commitment
- **Market precedent:** Kantata, Monday.com PSA, Kimble all use primarily sales-led motions
- Free trial inadequate to demonstrate value (requires 2-4 week pilot with actual team usage)

**Why PLG Doesn't Work Here:**
1. **High complexity:** PSA requires configuration for methodology, billing rules, resource planning
2. **Low self-serve adoption:** Users need guidance on best practices (time tracking, utilization reporting)
3. **Integration dependency:** Most SMBs already use QuickBooks, Stripe, or other tools ‚Üí need integration help
4. **Long evaluation cycle:** Can't understand value in 7-day free trial; needs 30-60 day pilot with real projects

**Founder Effort:** HIGH (building onboarding flows, self-serve tutorials, automated guidance)  
**Expected Conversion Rate:** 2-3% (very low for $12-25K ACV)  
**CAC Estimate:** $8,000-12,000 (through paid trial ads + content marketing)  
**Payback Period:** 15-18 months (unsustainable)

**Verdict:** ‚ùå **Avoid pure PLG.** Can add product-led elements (freemium tier, trial) after product-market fit established.

## 3. PSA SALES PROCESS DESIGN

### Sales Framework for DeliverySync

#### **Discovery Call (30 min) - Uncover Pain & Buying Signals**

**Goal:** Qualify ICP fit, understand pain severity, identify champion

**Key Questions:**

1. **Current State Problems:**
   - "How are you currently tracking time and projects?" (answer reveals manual work)
   - "What's the biggest pain point your team faces with time tracking?" (quantify: hours/week wasted)
   - "How long does month-end billing close take?" (target: >5 days = pain)

2. **Team & Process:**
   - "How many people are involved in resource planning?" (buying committee size)
   - "Who decides what tools your team uses?" (identify champion vs. decision-maker)
   - "What's your current tech stack?" (integration complexity check)

3. **Budget & Motivation:**
   - "Are you currently shopping for PSA solutions?" (buying signal)
   - "If the right tool saved your team 5-10 hours/week, what would that be worth?" (price tolerance)
   - "What's your timeline for a decision?" (sales cycle length)

4. **Success Criteria:**
   - "What would success look like 6 months in?" (identify key metrics)
   - "What needs to be true for you to say 'this is the right tool'?" (objections in advance)

**Qualification Criteria (SQL):**
- ‚úÖ Actively looking for PSA or admitting manual process pain
- ‚úÖ Budget $15-30K allocated (or can reallocate)
- ‚úÖ Team size 10-200 (fits ICP)
- ‚úÖ Decision cycle <3 months
- ‚úÖ Champion identified (ops person or PM, not just visitor)

**Disqualification Flags (Don't Demo):**
- ‚ùå "Just researching, no budget this year"
- ‚ùå "We use Kantata and love it" (competitor switching cost too high)
- ‚ùå Firm size <10 or >500 (outside ICP)
- ‚ùå "CEO decides tech" (long decision cycle, unlikely to sign)

## 5. GTM RISKS & MITIGATION

### Top 3 GTM Risks

#### **Risk #1: Slow Trial-to-Paid Conversion (Trial Drop-off)**

**Severity:** üî¥ **CRITICAL**

**Description:**
- Customer starts 30-day trial but doesn't see value by day 20
- Implementation takes longer than promised (data import, team setup)
- No "aha moment" (utilization report not working, integrations incomplete)
- Result: Trial expires, customer doesn't convert to paid

**Why It's Critical:**
- If trial-to-paid <40%, sales cycle extends to 10-12 weeks (kills velocity)
- Higher CAC because founder must do more discovery ‚Üí trial ‚Üí follow-up
- Compounds over time: 4 trials/month with 40% close = 1.6 new customers/month (slow growth)

**Probability:** 60% (high, given PSA onboarding complexity)

**Mitigation Strategy:**
1. **Dedicated Onboarding Process (First 5 Customers)**
   - Founder or CS team runs "Day 1 Onboarding Call" (30 min)
   - Purpose: Show how to do one time entry, explain setup
   - Prevent: Customer feels lost

2. **Day 15 Check-in Call (Non-Negotiable)**
   - Measure: Does customer have utilization report working? One project tracked?
   - If NO: Founder/CS helps troubleshoot immediately
   - If YES: Encourage customer to get team using for full week
   - Result: Course-correct before trial ends

3. **Trial Feature Gate (What Customers See by Day 20)**
   - ‚úÖ Time entry working
   - ‚úÖ Utilization report auto-generating
   - ‚úÖ Integration (QuickBooks/Asana) live
   - ‚ùå Hide: Advanced features (budget planning, resource forecasting)
   - Why: Show core value; complicate later

4. **Success Measurement Framework**
   - Metric: "By Day 20, customer has tracked 50+ hours of time"
   - If achieved: 80%+ trial-to-paid rate
   - If not achieved: Product/onboarding needs work before scaling

5. **Escalation Path**
   - Trial-to-paid <50%? Call all churned trials to understand
   - Document: "Why didn't customers convert?" (implementation, feature gap, replaced by competitor)
   - Iterate: Fix top 2 reasons

**Success Criteria:**
- Target: 60%+ trial-to-paid conversion rate
- Acceptable: 50-60% (vs. typical SaaS 20-30%)

---

#### **Risk #2: Competitive Displacement (Losing to Kantata, Float, Incumbents)**

**Severity:** üü† **HIGH**

**Description:**
- Target prospect: "We're already using Kantata. It's good, but pricey."
- Switching cost high: 2+ years of historical data, team trained on Kantata
- DeliverySync must prove 10x better on specific dimension (integration, ease, price, reporting)
- Result: Founder spends 8 weeks in sales cycle, loses to incumbent in final week

**Why It's High Risk:**
- Kantata has 10+ year head start, deep feature set
- Float has existing user base of light-weight fans
- Monday.com has massive brand + ecosystem
- Buyer resistance to switching (risk aversion, retraining cost)

**Probability:** 70% (very likely in SMB market)

**Mitigation Strategy:**

1. **"Kantata Alternative" Positioning**
   - Messaging: "Same features as Kantata for 40% less, built for 10-50 person firms"
   - Blog post: "Kantata vs. DeliverySync: Feature Comparison + Pricing"
   - Demo script: "Here's what you're paying for in Kantata that you don't need..."

2. **Free Data Migration Offer**
   - "Worried about switching? We'll migrate your Kantata data for free. No manual work."
   - Remove #1 switching barrier
   - Build $3-5K value prop ("data migration cost")

3. **Niche Differentiation (Pick One)**
   - Option A: "Built for agencies" (design/marketing firms)
     - Positioning: "We understand creative agency billing; Kantata is generic"
     - Proof: 70%+ of case studies = creative agencies
   
   - Option B: "Built for consulting" (management/tax/strategy firms)
     - Positioning: "Consulting workflows are unique; we bake in best practices"
     - Proof: Integration with Big 4 consulting methodologies
   
   - Option C: "Built for remote teams" (distributed PSA)
     - Positioning: "Global time zone tracking, simplified for remote"
     - Proof: Async time entry, timezone-smart reporting

4. **Low-Friction Comparison Calls**
   - "If you're evaluating vs. Kantata, let me spend 20 min showing you what we do differently"
   - Stack-rank on: Price, ease of use, specific feature (e.g., utilization reporting)
   - Help prospect make informed decision (even if they pick Kantata)
   - Why: Build goodwill; some will choose DeliverySync; rest become advocates

5. **Customer Success & Retention**
   - Satisfy early customers deeply
   - Encourage case studies + referrals
   - Reduce churn to <5%/month (early stage)
   - Word-of-mouth will compete with Kantata's marketing budget

**Success Criteria:**
- 15%+ of prospects come from "Kantata Alternative" searches
- 10-20% of deals are Kantata replacement (customers switching)
- Customer retention >95%/year (vs. typical SaaS 80-90%)

#### **Risk #3: Founder Burnout & Sales Cycle Bottleneck**

**Severity:** üü† **HIGH**

**Description:**
- Founder is doing 25-30 hours/week of founder-led sales (discovery, demo, close)
- Also managing product, engineering, customer success
- By month 4-5: Founder exhausted, demos slow down, sales stall
- Hiring first sales person (BDR/SDR) takes 4-8 weeks (recruiting, onboarding)
- Result: 6-8 week gap where sales falls off; misses $150K ARR target

**Why It's High Risk:**
- Founder's time is finite resource
- Sales ramp requires consistent volume (not possible if founder checks out)
- Hiring/training SDR takes 8 weeks before they generate leads
- Creates death spiral: Founder burns out ‚Üí Sales drop ‚Üí Revenue drops ‚Üí Can't hire sales team

**Probability:** 50-60% (very common for founder-led sales in early stage)

**Mitigation Strategy:**

1. **Hire First Sales Person Early (Month 3-4)**
   - Don't wait until revenue is high
   - Hire BDR/SDR to do outreach (founder does discovery + demo)
   - BDR role: 100 LinkedIn outreach/week, qualify, book discovery calls
   - Reduce founder demand from 25 hrs ‚Üí 15 hrs/week

2. **Sales Process Documentation (Month 1-2)**
   - Document every step:
     * ICP definition + list
     * Outreach templates (LI, email, follow-up)
     * Discovery call script (top 10 questions)
     * Demo flow (key slides, talking points)
     * Objection playbook (common responses)
   - Why: Enables handoff to BDR/SDR without losing quality

3. **Batch Demo Days (Founder Productivity)**
   - Instead of 1 demo/day scattered across week, batch to 2 days/week
   - Monday-Tuesday: 4-5 demos in a row (founder prepared, focused)
   - Wed-Fri: Admin, product, customer support
   - Result: Founder does same volume, fewer context switches

4. **Sales Metrics & Alerts**
   - Track weekly: # outreach, # meetings booked, # demos done
   - If metrics drop 20%+ week-over-week, trigger escalation
   - Action: Founder takes 1-week "sales sprint" to refresh

5. **Sustainability Check (Monthly)**
   - Founder self-assessment: "Can I sustain this pace for 6 more months?"
   - If NO: Immediately hire part-time sales help or reduce product roadmap
   - Burnout is not a badge of honor; it kills startups

**Success Criteria:**
- Founder can sustain 15-20 hours/week on sales for 12 months
- No burnout-induced gaps in sales cycle
- By month 9, BDR/SDR taking 40% of outreach/discovery load
